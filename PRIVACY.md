# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-20_

This Privacy Policy explains how **CyberSnatcher** (the "Software") handles your
information. The short version: **it doesn't collect any.**

> **Not legal advice.** This document is provided for general informational
> purposes and is not legal advice. It was not drafted by a lawyer.

---

## The short version

**Feris (the author) does not collect, store, receive, transmit, sell, share, or
have access to any information about the people who use this tool — none at all.**
Everything the Software does happens locally, inside your own browser, on your
own device.

## We do not collect your data

CyberSnatcher has **no servers, no accounts, no analytics, and no tracking.**
Specifically, the Software does **not**:

- ❌ Collect or store personal information (no name, email, address, etc.);
- ❌ Track your browsing history, the pages you visit, or what you download;
- ❌ Use cookies, fingerprinting, analytics, or telemetry of any kind;
- ❌ Send any data to the author, to Anthropic, or to any third party;
- ❌ Show ads or share/sell information with advertisers;
- ❌ Contain any "phone-home," auto-update, or remote-code mechanism.

Because there is no server behind the Software, there is simply **nowhere for
your data to go** and **no one receiving it.** The author cannot see what you
detect, download, or do with the Software.

## How the Software actually works

- All detection, processing, and downloading happens **entirely on your own
  device**, within your browser.
- When you download media, your browser communicates **directly with the same
  website/video server the page is already using** — exactly as your browser
  normally would. The author is never in the middle of that connection.
- Anything the Software needs to remember (such as the list of media detected on
  your current tab, or the progress of an active download) is kept in your
  browser's **temporary local session storage** and is **discarded automatically**
  when you navigate away or close the tab. It never leaves your device.
- Files you download are saved by **your own browser's standard download
  feature**, to a location you control.

## Browser permissions (and why they're needed)

The Software requests certain Chrome permissions purely to do its job locally —
never to gather information about you:

- **Access to the sites you visit** — so it can detect media on the page you
  currently have open. It cannot know in advance which site you'll use it on, so
  it must be permitted to read the active page. It uses this **only** to find
  media on your current tab, and only while you are using it.
- **Downloads** — to save the media file to your device through the browser.
- **Storage** — to temporarily hold the detected-media list and download progress
  on your device (see above).
- **Notifications** — to tell you when a download finishes.

These permissions are used solely for the Software's stated function. None of
them are used to collect, transmit, or monetize your information.

## Third parties

The Software does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, tracking,
or data-broker services. The only external connections it makes are the direct
requests to the media servers of the website **you** choose to use it on, in
order to download the content **you** select.

## Children's privacy

The Software does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including
children. Because it collects no data at all, it does not target or profile
users of any age.

## Your control

Because no data is collected, there is nothing for the author to access, export,
correct, or delete on your behalf. You remain in full control: you can uninstall
the extension at any time from your browser's extensions page, which removes the
Software and any temporary local data it used.
